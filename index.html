In a few words

I got my PhD on physical chemistry from the Université Bordeaux in 2008 under the supervision of Erick Dufourc. Then, I went to the Université de Picardie Jules Verne as a postdoctoral fellow before obtaining a position. Since September 2009, I am a maître de conférences (assistant professor) at the UPJV and joined the Génie Enzymatique et Cellulaire for the research part.

My main research interest is the understanding of how small molecules, such as (lipo)peptides, interact with lipid model membranes and alter (or disrupts) the membrane dynamics. I am particularly interested in combining experimental methods (NMR, DLS, EM...) with Molecular Dynamics simulations. To do that, I develop software tools to extract physical parameters from MD simulations.

I teach mainly biochemistry (bachelor students) and biophysics (master students).
